Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us

Last Updated: November 19, 2025

TL;DR: MarkView does NOT collect, store, or transmit any of your personal data. All your settings stay on your device. We respect your privacy completely.

1. Introduction

Welcome to MarkView. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our Chrome extension ("MarkView", "Extension", "we", "us", or "our").

MarkView is designed with privacy as a core principle. We believe your markdown files and browsing habits are your business, not ours.

2. Information We Do NOT Collect

MarkView does NOT collect, store, transmit, or share:

  • Your markdown file contents
  • Your browsing history or URLs visited
  • Personal identification information (name, email, etc.)
  • Usage statistics or analytics
  • IP addresses or device information
  • Any form of tracking or behavioral data

3. Local Data Storage

MarkView uses Chrome's local storage API (chrome.storage.local) to save your preferences on your device only. This data includes:

  • Theme selection (light/dark/auto)
  • Font family choice
  • Font size and line height settings
  • Layout preferences (centered mode, max width)
  • Feature toggles (syntax highlighting, auto-refresh, etc.)

Important: All this data stays on your device and is never transmitted anywhere. You can clear this data anytime through Chrome's extension settings.

4. Permissions Explained

MarkView requests the following permissions to function properly:

4.1 Storage Permission

Why needed: To save your preferences (theme, font, layout) locally on your device.

Data access: Only reads/writes settings data on your device. No external transmission.

4.2 ActiveTab Permission

Why needed: To access the markdown file content in the currently active tab so we can render it beautifully.

Data access: Only accesses the markdown file you're viewing. Processing happens entirely in your browser.

4.3 File URL Access (file:///)

Why needed: To read and render local markdown files (.md files on your computer).

Data access: Only accesses files when you explicitly open them. Requires manual activation in extension settings.

Note: This permission must be manually enabled by you in Chrome's extension settings.

4.4 Host Permissions (*://*/)

Why needed: To fetch external markdown files from GitHub, GitLab, and other websites for the auto-refresh feature.

Data access: Only fetches the specific markdown file URL you're viewing, and only when auto-refresh is enabled.

5. Third-Party Services

5.1 Google Fonts

MarkView loads custom fonts from Google Fonts CDN (fonts.googleapis.com) to provide beautiful typography options. When you use a custom font:

  • Your browser makes a request to Google's servers to download the font files
  • Google may log this request as part of their standard CDN operations
  • This is a standard web service used by millions of websites
  • Google's privacy policy applies to this service

Note: If you select the "Default" font option, no external fonts are loaded.

5.2 External Markdown Sources

When you view markdown files from external sources (GitHub, GitLab, etc.):

  • Your browser fetches the file directly from that source
  • MarkView does not proxy or store these files
  • The privacy policies of those platforms apply
  • No data about your viewing is sent to MarkView

6. Data Security

Since MarkView doesn't collect or transmit your data, there's no central database or server that could be compromised. Your data security is as strong as your device's security.

All processing happens locally in your browser using standard Chrome extension APIs.

7. Children's Privacy

MarkView does not collect any information from anyone, including children under 13. The extension is safe for users of all ages.

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in:

  • Our extension's features
  • Legal or regulatory requirements
  • Industry best practices

When we make changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page
  • Notify users through the extension update notes (if significant changes)

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.

9. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your data: All your settings are stored locally in Chrome. You can view them in chrome://extensions → MarkView → Storage
  • Delete your data: Right-click the extension icon → Remove from Chrome, or clear site data in Chrome settings
  • Opt-out: Simply don't use the extension, or disable specific features in settings

10. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), California residents have specific rights regarding their personal information. However, since MarkView does not collect personal information, these rights are not applicable.

11. European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), European users have specific rights regarding their personal data. However, since MarkView does not collect personal information, GDPR obligations regarding data collection, storage, and processing do not apply.

Although we don't collect personal data, the local storage of preferences is based on:

15. Disclaimer

MarkView is provided "as-is" without any warranties. While we take privacy seriously, users are responsible for:

  • Securing their own devices
  • Being aware of what markdown files they open
  • Understanding that external markdown sources (GitHub, etc.) have their own privacy policies

Summary

MarkView respects your privacy completely. We don't collect your data. We don't track you. We don't sell anything. We're just here to make your markdown files look beautiful. Your files, your settings, your privacy – all stay with you.
